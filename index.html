type history

Fonts that changed everything.

Old Style · Renaissance

Garamond

c. 1530 · Claude Garamond

Claude Garamond's types became the foundation for European book printing for over a century. Elegant, readable, and utterly human — cut from the same tradition as the Italian humanist hand. Still one of the most widely used typefaces in the world, which either means it's timeless or everyone's out of ideas.

Didone · Neoclassical

Bodoni

c. 1798 · Giambattista Bodoni

Bodoni pushed type to the edge of legibility in pursuit of pure geometry — extreme contrast between thick and thin strokes, mathematical precision. Fashion magazines have never recovered. Neither has anyone who's tried to set it at small sizes. It is, objectively, too beautiful to be practical.

Geometric Sans · Modernist

Futura

1927 · Paul Renner

Designed for the future, which is funny because it's been the past for a century and still looks more modern than half of what gets released today. Geometric circles and triangles masquerading as letters. Used on the moon. Volkswagen. Ikea. Your friend's brand that "just does clean design."

apologies for having opinions about kerning

fonts matter. started in San Diego, which means we grew up next to the Pacific Ocean and still found time to have strong feelings about typefaces. priorities.

this obsession with typography didn't start last year. it's been thirty years of noticing the wrong font on a sign, the bad kerning on a menu, the moment a brand's whole personality collapses because someone picked Arial. we've been the person at the table saying that's not the right typeface since before it was something you could say out loud.

and it's not just one of us. there are more people behind this than you'd expect for a brand that talks about fonts for a living. some of us design. some of us write. some of us just show up and say the tracking is off. all of us mean it.
